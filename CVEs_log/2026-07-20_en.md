Node`s native WHATWG URL parser, used by fetch, undici, and Node`s http and https clients, normalizes the backslash to a forward slash for special schemes such as http, https, ws, wss, ftp, and file. Applications that use fast-uri to enforce host-based policy such as allowlists, denylists, loopback or SSRF filtering, redirect validation, or outbound proxy routing before passing the same URL into Node`s URL or fetch consumers can be steered to an unintended destination, including cloud metadata endpoints, loopback, or internal hosts.
